Ground truth, per inverter

We catch the kWh you didn't know you were losing.

Every inverter is compared against its own fleet, so weather cancels out — a passing cloud dims all of them equally, but a dead string only dims one. That's how EnergyAgent spots trouble the day it starts, not on next year's true-up.

  • Dead strings and tripped optimizers
  • Shaded or soiled panels dragging output
  • Comm gaps where a site quietly stops reporting

Then we tell you the cost in plain language: not "DC fault 0x21" — "this inverter is down ~9 kWh/day, about $1.40."
